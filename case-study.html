Case Study

GTM Strategy for Vutto

GTM Strategy  ·  2025  ·  Seed stage used 2-Wheeler startup

01 — Problem

People couldn’t understand in the first glance what the company did from the website or the showroom. The name “Vutto” wasn’t self-explanatory and had nothing to do with used two wheelers for most people, as compared to competitors like Cars24. The website and signboard said “Vutto: Quality Bikes, Best Price.”

02 — Insight

We were dealing with an audience with a lower economic and educational background, that could have difficulty in understanding complex terms. During customer interviews, we discovered that 25% of people expressed confusion on whether the bikes were new or old. Also, 34% of people did not remember what Vutto was, despite being in the store or having signed up on the website.

03 — Strategy

Change all physical and digital assets to “Vutto: Buy, Sell and Exchange Used Two Wheelers”

04 — Execution

05 — Impact
+15% Walk-ins
−10% Cost per lead
+4000% Organic traffic

The repositioned messaging became the foundation for all future marketing, and was adopted by the product & sales team across all communication.

06 — Additional Points

Changing this on Google Maps listings also brought in significant online and offline visitors, as it helped in SEO.

Most brands promising quality don’t explicitly say it as “quality”. That had to be conveyed differently, through the premium appearance of the brand and products.
